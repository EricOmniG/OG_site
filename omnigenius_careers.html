Why work here

This company was built from a warehouse floor and a question that had no answer yet.

We didn't start with funding. We didn't start with a team of PhDs. We started with three research papers, a lunch break, and the kind of stubborn curiosity that refuses to accept "that's not how it works" as a final answer.

That's the spirit we'll hire into. We want people who build because they have to — not because it's a good career move. People who get genuinely uncomfortable when something doesn't make sense. People who would rather be embarrassingly wrong and learn from it than safely correct and stagnate.

The work is hard, real, and unfinished. We're building a model architecture that doesn't exist anywhere else, a training methodology that challenges consensus, and a product people are genuinely excited about. There's a lot left to do — and most of it is still undefined.

What we look for
🧩
Genuinely curious
You read papers for fun. You pull on threads that don't need to be pulled. You can't leave a confusing thing unresolved.
🔨
Builder first
You default to making something rather than planning to make something. Speed of iteration matters more to you than perfection on the first try.
🎯
Honest about what you don't know
You'd rather say "I don't know, let me find out" than bluff confidence. Epistemic honesty isn't a virtue here — it's a baseline.
🌊
Comfortable with ambiguity
Early-stage means the roadmap is partly made of fog. You navigate that well — by shipping, learning, and adjusting.
⚡
High output, low drama
You do a lot with few resources and you don't make the work harder than it needs to be. No politics, no posturing.
🧠
Strong opinions, loosely held
You have a point of view and you'll defend it — until someone shows you a better one. Then you update immediately and move on.
